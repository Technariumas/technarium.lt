Join Technarium!

Technarium is an independent hackerspace/workshop based in Vilnius, Lithuania. We are a non-commercial community initiative sustained by membership fees we use to cover the rent and other expenses.

If you would like to become a member, contact us: we'll show you around. In order to accomodate all different needs and abilities, we offer the following membership levels starting on June 2016:

0. Visitor/starving hacker membership -- 10€/month.
For those who just hand out here occasionally or who cannot afford the standard membership at a given moment. Also for those who like the idea of an open community working space and would like to support us. Visitor members do not have the keys to Technarium and are welcome when other members are at the space. They can also use some hand-held tools, the common area, kitchen and wireless Internet access.
1. Standard membership -- 30€/month.
Members can get a key to the space, some storage place for their stuff, use the tools and equipment in the workshops (wood, metal, electronics, 3D-printing, etc.). Use of some equipment, such as welders, machines, 3D printers, ceramics kiln, etc. is only allowed after training.
2. Own Corner -- 35€/month + 30€/month for each person.
A personal working space in Technarium for you and your friends. Some working space you don't have to tidy up before leaving + storage space.
3. Freelancer membership -- minimum 50€/month, depending on work type.
For those who would be working at Technarium and use the tools and equipment on a daily basis.
4. A one-off large project -- 150€/month.
For those who would like to work on their large one-off project at Technarium. You will be provided with work and storage space, an opportunity to use tools and equipment, some advice and training if needed.

We are a community, not landlords. The community and people with their ideas make this place come alive and are more important than finance. If you cannot afford full membership but would like to make something useful or interesting for everybody, let's talk. We shall never close the door to good initiatives because of money.
